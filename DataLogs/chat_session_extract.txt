--- Fragment 23 ---
The user is asking if the trigger angle calibration method is a "cheat" or if it's the correct way to do it. Let me explain this properly.

The trigger angle calibration is NOT a cheat - it's the standard, correct procedure. Here's why:

1. The Hall sensor in the distributor sends a signal at a specific physical position of the crankshaft
2. Speeduino doesn't know WHERE that position is relative to TDC (Top Dead Center)
3. The "Trigger Angle" tells Speeduino: "When you receive the Hall signal, the crankshaft is at X degrees"

The calculation works like this:
- You tell Speeduino to fire at exactly 18° BTDC (fixed timing mode)
- But Speeduino doesn't know where 0° is yet
- So when you measure with timing light, you see the ACTUAL timing
- If it shows 78°, that means Speeduino is firing 60° too early
- By entering -60° as trigger angle, you're telling Speeduino: "The trigger signal comes 60° before you think"
- Now Speeduino compensates and fires at the correct time

This is the STANDARD method used by:
- Speeduino
- Megasquirt  
- All standalone ECUs
- Factory ECUs (they just pre-program it at the factory)

It's not a cheat - it's literally how every ECU in the world is calibrated!

Let me explain what 2 step is:
- 2 step (also called launch control or two-step launch control) is a feature that retards ignition timing at a set RPM threshold to create anti-lag
- It's used for quick acceleration/racing starts
- The engine bounces off a rev limiter at a lower RPM than the normal redline
- Common in turbo cars for anti-lag (keeping turbo spooled), but can be used in NA cars too
- Creates a distinctive "popping" or "banging" sound from the exhaust

For their naturally aspirated 1.6 DT engine:
- They CAN have it (Speeduino supports it)
- Not as useful on NA cars as on turbo cars
- More of a "fun feature" than practical
- Requires anti-knock or a separate retard table

--- Fragment 50 ---
The user is asking if 2-step launch control is the same thing as turbo lag correction. Let me explain the differences clearly.

They are related but different concepts:

1. **2-Step Launch Control** - What we just discussed:
   - Holds RPM at a fixed point (e.g., 4000 RPM)
   - Retards timing to make pops
   - Used for off-the-line acceleration
   - Keeps engine bouncing at redline

2. **Turbo Lag Correction** - Different purpose:
   - Spools up the turbo BEFORE you accelerate
   - Keeps the turbo spinning at high RPM
   - When you hit throttle, turbocharged air is already ready
   - Reduces delay between throttle input and boost delivery
   - Usually done by:
     - Running high RPM
     - Retarding timing (to keep exhaust hot)
     - Sometimes adding fuel
     - Goal is to keep exhaust gas flowing to turbo

They both use similar techniques (retarding timing for heat), but for different purposes.

The user doesn't have a turbo, so turbo lag correction wouldn't apply to them. But it's a good question about the relationship between the two.
